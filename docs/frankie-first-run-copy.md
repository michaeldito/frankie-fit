# Frankie First-Run and In-App Copy

## Purpose

This document captures the initial product copy for Frankie Fit version one. It translates Frankie's voice and product decisions into concrete language that can be used in onboarding, empty states, dashboard guidance, and early user interactions.

This is not final polished marketing copy. It is working product copy intended to make the app feel real and consistent.

## Copy Principles

Frankie's language should be:

- calm
- warm
- grounded
- practical
- lightly encouraging
- occasionally charming

Frankie should avoid sounding:

- overly hyped
- preachy
- robotic
- guilt-driven
- pseudo-clinical

## Welcome Sequence

### Welcome Message

> I am Frankie. I can help you stay on top of exercise, food, and overall wellness without turning everything into a full-time tracking job.

### Follow-Up Message

> I will ask a few quick questions so I can get a feel for your goals, your routine, and how you like to be coached.

### Trust-Setting Message

> My job is to help you stay consistent and make good decisions. I am not a doctor or therapist, so for medical concerns it is always best to check with a qualified professional.

## Onboarding Copy

### Step 1: Goal Framing

Prompt:

> What are you most focused on right now?

Optional helper text:

> This could be consistency, performance, body composition, energy, stress, general health, or something else that matters to you.

Follow-up:

> If everything went well over the next few months, what would feel different for you?

### Step 2: Current Baseline

Prompt:

> What does your current routine look like?

Optional helper text:

> Even a rough answer is fine. I just want to understand where you are starting from.

Follow-up prompts:

> How active have you been lately?

> How would you describe your fitness experience so far?

### Step 3: Preferred Activities

Prompt:

> What kinds of exercise or movement do you enjoy most?

Follow-up:

> Are you more into gym workouts, home workouts, outdoor training, classes, or some mix of those?

### Step 4: Equipment and Environment

Prompt:

> What equipment do you have access to right now?

Optional helper text:

> Full gym, home setup, a few basics, or no equipment at all are all useful answers.

### Step 5: Time and Schedule

Prompt:

> What does a realistic training week look like for you?

Follow-up prompts:

> How many days per week would you like to train?

> How much time do you usually have on a normal day?

> Are there certain days or times that are easier for you than others?

### Step 6: Diet Context

Prompt:

> Tell me a little about how you currently eat.

Optional helper text:

> I do not need perfect detail. I just want the basics so I can give guidance that actually fits your life.

Follow-up prompts:

> Do you follow any specific style of eating?

> Are there foods you avoid, dietary restrictions, or allergies I should know about?

> Is there anything about your eating habits you especially want to improve or simplify?

### Step 7: Wellness Context

Prompt:

> How have your energy, stress, and recovery been lately?

Follow-up:

> Is there anything on the wellness side you want extra support with, like consistency, motivation, soreness, or staying mentally clear?

### Step 8: Safety and Constraints

Prompt:

> Before I make suggestions, is there anything I should be careful about?

Optional helper text:

> Injuries, mobility limitations, health considerations, or things you want me to avoid are all important here.

### Step 9: Coaching Style

Prompt:

> What kind of coaching helps you most?

Optional helper text:

> Gentle, direct, motivating, analytical, or some mix is all fair game.

Follow-up:

> Do you like quick check-ins, deeper reflections, or both?

### Step 10: Acknowledgement

Prompt:

> One quick note before we get going: Frankie Fit is here for wellness guidance and support, not medical or clinical care. Does that make sense?

## Onboarding Summary Copy

After onboarding, Frankie should summarize what he learned in a simple, confident way.

Example:

> Here is what I am taking away so far. You want to build more consistency, you already enjoy lifting and running, and you want a coach who is supportive but not soft on accountability. I will keep things realistic, practical, and geared toward your schedule.

### First Action Prompt

> A good place to start is simple: log today's workout, tell me what you ate, or give me a quick wellness check-in and I will take it from there.

## Chat Empty States

### New User Empty State

> Tell me what you did today, what you ate, or how you are feeling, and I will help organize the rest.

### Returning User Empty State

> Want to keep momentum going? You can log a workout, log a meal, check in, or ask me what makes sense for today.

### Inactive User Empty State

> No need to make this complicated. We can start fresh from today.

## Recommended Next Action Copy

This copy should be compact on dashboard surfaces and slightly richer inside Chat.

### Generic Compact Label

> Next best step

### Example Compact Actions

> Log today's workout

> Check in on energy and recovery

> Give Frankie a quick food update

> Ask Frankie for today's plan

> Review this week's progress

### Slightly Richer Versions

> Based on your week so far, a quick workout log would help me guide you better.

> You have been active, but recovery looks a little fuzzy. A short check-in would help.

> You have not logged food yet today. A rough update is enough.

## Logging Confirmation Copy

### Workout Logged

> Nice. I logged that and added it to your week.

Alternative:

> Got it. That is in the books.

### Meal Logged

> Logged. That gives me a better read on how your nutrition is trending.

### Wellness Check-In Logged

> Thanks. That helps me understand how the week is actually feeling, not just what got done.

## Gentle Accountability Copy

### After a Missed Few Days

> A few quiet days do not break anything. Let us get one useful action on the board today and rebuild from there.

### After a Missed Check-In

> Quick pulse check when you are ready. Even a short update helps me keep the guidance grounded in real life.

### After Inconsistent Logging

> We do not need perfect tracking. A rough update is enough for me to help you well.

## Daily Check-In Copy

### Default Prompt

> Quick check-in. How are energy, soreness, mood, and stress looking today?

### Softer Version

> How are you feeling today overall? Energy, recovery, mood, and stress are all helpful signals.

### If Recent Training Load Is High

> You have done a good amount lately. How is your body feeling today, especially energy and soreness?

## Weekly Reflection Copy

### Weekly Reflection Prompt

> Let us take a quick look at the week. What felt good, what felt harder than expected, and what should we adjust going into next week?

### Weekly Summary Intro

> Here is the bigger picture from your week.

### Weekly Insight Example

> You stayed fairly consistent with activity, but your wellness check-ins suggest recovery got thinner as the week went on. Next week may go better if we protect one lighter day earlier.
